EVENTS

Academic Excellence Awards

To recognise and encourage academic achievement, the CODE Fair begins with a brief award ceremony recognising the highest achieving students in IT units for the past 12 months. Supported by the NT Department of Corporate Information Services, this initiative enables the university to also reach out to our external students who are excelling academically and provide them an opportunity to visit CDU campus and meet some of their peers.

Hour of Code Workshops

Supporting the global hour of code movement, workshops for schools and university staff are held at the University on the day of the Code Fair or prior to the code fair, at schools locally. Register to attend on the day or at your school.

Employer Speed Dating

Industry Employer Speed Dating provides graduating students with an opportunity to meet local employers to kick start their career. 20 places are available. Preference is given to students in the final year of their degrees and to students who complete the preparation requirements below.

Sign Up

PREPARATION

To be eligible to be part of the event, students need to undertake a number of preparation tasks.

  • 1. Students need to create a Linked In page listing their past employment experience.

  • 2. Students need to create an About Me 1 minute video that outlines their IT passions, skills and experience. This should be displayed on their Linked In page and completed no later than Nov 5. This information will be circulated to prospective employers at least 4 days prior

  • 3. Students participating in the Employer Speed Dating must attend a workshop on the Monday prior to the Code Fair to discuss your 1 minute pitch and receive feedback on your CV.

On The Day

  • • Wear professional dress or your Code Fair Tshirts (if you have participated in the event).

    ,/li>

  • • Arrive at least 10 minutes early.

  • • Have a one-page CV document that can be left with prospective employers.
