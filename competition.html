COMPETITIONS

Business Innovation Challenge

The Business Innovation Challenge is provided by the NT government. Students are presented with a real-life problem related to current issue.

Students are challenged provide a solution:

  • • Students are to create/ develop a working prototype (code) and or a concept (video or poster) for solving the problem

  • • Communicate to a panel of judges in a 5-minute presentation

Win up to $ {to be filled}.

Students may have the opportunity to be employed, turning their ideas into reality.

Coding Challenge

A Coding competition is open to all IT students (VET and Higher Education) who have created software, apps, programs, mobile prototypes, websites, games that demonstrate their developing coding knowledge.

  • • A panel of industry judges decide the best projects

  • • Various categories are available for individual or team projects.

  • • Coding experience determines levels: novice, intermediate and advanced

SIGN UP

Networking Challenge

A new competition, the Networking Challenge is for all IT students who would enjoy tackling network related issues arising daily. These may include security problems, maintenance, and monitoring of network traffic and network performance issues.

For this challenge students need to solve a problem and communicate the solution in a thorough 5 minute presentation that includes a 90 seconds video that delivers a high level overview of the solution.

Poster Challenge

The Poster competition encourages students whose IT future career pathways are more inclined towards the analytical aspects of profession. Communicating IT work and developing and expanding visual communication skills is an important twenty first century skill for most professions.

SIGN UP

Digital Storytelling Challenge

Digital Storytelling combines narrative with digital content, (including images, sound, and video,) to create a short movie (no more than 90 seconds for this challenge). Digital stories can be instructional, persuasive, historical, or reflective. In Information Technology, digital storytelling can be used to prototype innovative ideas, sell new products, onboard new users to a new product or simply just provide more information about students.

Create a digital story between 90 - 120 seconds and enter the competition. For the first year, there is only one category and open to everyone to have a go.
